What Is a Data Breach?

A data breach occurs when a company fails to adequately protect sensitive consumer information — including names, emails, financial records, medical data, and login credentials — allowing unauthorized parties to access or steal it.

Identity Theft Risk

Exposed personal data can be used to open fraudulent accounts, file false tax returns, or access your financial accounts.

Time and Stress

Victims spend hundreds of hours resolving fraud, disputing charges, and monitoring their credit in the aftermath.

Loss of Privacy

Once your data is exposed, it can circulate on the dark web indefinitely, creating ongoing risk for years.
